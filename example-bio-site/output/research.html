Wetware Computing vs. Silicon Clusters: The Impending Energy Efficiency Paradigm Shift

As generative AI models scale exponentially, the carbon footprint and raw power demands of traditional silicon-based GPU clusters are reaching critical limitations.

This research draft evaluates the theoretical and practical boundaries of emerging wetware computing compared to standard cloud-based GPU nodes.

Inquiry Vectors

Two directions shaping the research agenda.

The work examines both infrastructure-level energy economics and the software architecture needed to enforce deterministic behaviour in non-traditional compute models.

Vector 01

Thermal and power differentials

It contrasts the human brain's ability to execute massive multi-modal inference tasks on roughly 20 watts of power against megawatt-level hyperscale data centres running dense Transformer architectures.

Vector 02

Deterministic alignment in biological models

It explores the structural software architecture needed to translate biological or semi-biological processing states into predictable outputs aligned with enterprise compliance and guardrail standards.
