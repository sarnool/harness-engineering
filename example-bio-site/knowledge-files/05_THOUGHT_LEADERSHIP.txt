TITLE: Wetware Computing vs. Silicon Clusters: The Impending Energy Efficiency Paradigm Shift

ABSTRACT:
As generative AI models scale exponentially, the carbon footprint and raw power demands of traditional silicon-based GPU clusters are reaching critical limitations. This research draft evaluates the theoretical and practical boundaries of emerging wetware computing (biological processors) compared to standard cloud-based GPU nodes.

KEY INQUIRY VECTOR 1: THERMAL AND POWER DIFFERENTIALS
Analysis of the human brain's ability to execute massive multi-modal inference tasks on roughly 20 watts of power, contrasted against megawatt-level hyperscale data centers running dense Transformer architectures.

KEY INQUIRY VECTOR 2: DETERMINISTIC ALIGNMENT IN BIOLOGICAL MODELS
Exploring the structural software architecture needed to translate biological or semi-biological processing states into predictable, deterministic outputs that safely align with enterprise compliance and guardrail standards.
